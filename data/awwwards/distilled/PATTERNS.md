## Key findings for Cipher training

1. **Motion is required.** Lenis in 16.7%, GSAP detected in 17.7% (true usage per submitter tags: **47.9%**). The modal Awwwards motion stack is `GSAP + ScrollTrigger + SplitText + Lenis` (`ravi-klaassens` pattern).

2. **WebGL/3D is a top-tier signal.** Submitter tags show **WebGL 38.5%** and **Three.js 30.2%** of winners. Our HTML detection finds fewer because libraries are bundled. Cipher should feel confident shipping inline Three.js for hero moments.

3. **Framework split:** Nuxt/Vue (24.0%), Webflow (21.9%), Next.js (15.6%), vanilla/undetected (22.9%). Our single-file HTML maps best to the vanilla cohort.

4. **Fonts:** **Inter** (2), **Geist Mono** (2), **Amatic SC** (1), **Instrument Serif** (1), **Google Sans Text** (1), **Fragment Mono** (1). Sans-serif Inter dominates; serif/display (Fraunces, Instrument Serif, Playfair) used for editorial headlines.

5. **Modern CSS is standard**, not experimental: `@keyframes` (78), `clamp()` (43), `aspect-ratio` (76), `grid-template` (77), `mix-blend-mode` (42), `backdrop-filter` (56). Emerging: `:has()` (37), `color-mix()` (23), `oklch()` (10).

## Training implications

For Stage 2.5 (second SFT on gold) and Stage 3 (GRPO):

- **Inject the canonical motion stack in Cipher's system prompt.** The `GSAP + ScrollTrigger + SplitText + Lenis` quartet is the single most reliable Awwwards signal. Always include their CDN tags unless the prompt says otherwise.
- **Reward bonus for Three.js/WebGL on prompts that warrant it.** Any time the prompt mentions hero, immersive, experience, 3D, shader, particles — absence of `<canvas>` or Three.js should score lower.
- **Reward modern CSS.** Penalize plain `rgb()` if the site has a palette spec; bonus for `oklch()`, `color-mix()`, `clamp()` for type scales, and `@container` for responsive components.
- **Reward structure.** At least 3 sections, a nav, a footer, semantic landmarks.
- **Section target:** mean of **4.6** `<section>` elements per site.
- **Font pairing:** serif-for-display + Inter-for-body is the Awwwards archetype. Penalize outputs that use a single font for everything unless the prompt is minimal.
